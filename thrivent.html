Project

Initial task:

“Make the Life Insurance experience better”

Okay... but what does that mean, exactly?

So we dove into research.

Interviews

We interviewed dozens of Thrivent members, as well as people who signed up with other Insurance companies.

Competitive Analysis

There is a lot of competition, so we looked at how others stack up.

Heuristic Analysis

We used existing best practices to identify where the current experience could improve.

And synthesized our findings.

We built a service design blueprint representing Thrivent's current Life Insurance touch-points. Assisted by our heuristic analysis and interviews with customers and employees, we had in-depth understanding of the process.

Combining our blueprint with competitive analyses, we found several areas for improvement, like:

  • Help financial advisors more effectively parse through applications
  • Enable paperless documentation
  • Improve communication lines between relevant parties


Research + business priorities + team scope =


Digitize the Term Life Insurance application process

Allow users to complete it on their own

Clarify confusing questions/instructions

Reduce the number of errors in applications

Here are some examples of the redesigned application process:





Improved Expectation-Setting

Hover over Before and After images to see expanded information.

Before

Links to fill out the form provided no contextual information (like duration of the application, additional materials needed, etc.), so financial advisors have to do the heavy lifting.

After

The new form starts with a landing screen that indicates important information before starting, like the expected duration, the topics of questions, what materials to have handy.


Additionally, we include helpful guidance on next steps after filling out the form.

User-friendly Order of Questions

Hover over Before and After images to see expanded information.

Before

The original form asked particularly invasive questions early on, which can scare users away.

After

The new form is organized in three sections, starting with less-personal questions to ease users in.

Conditional Questions

Hover over Before and After images to see expanded information.

Before

Some questions are only required based on previous questions’ responses. In the Docusign/paper experience, users had to read a lot of instructional text to determine which which questions are actually required.


Additionally, they needed to jump around on the form out of order to properly fill it out.

After

All the conditional logic is handled by the system, saving users from the added cognitive load of complicated instructional text. This also decreases errors, saving time for all parties.

Added Answer Validations

Hover over Before and After images to see expanded information.

Before

The old version had no limitations how a user could answer a question, leading to common mistakes.


The only validation was for required questions, but couldn’t identify more complex errors.

After

In our new flow, questions have far more validations to ensure accurate reporting (such as accidentally entering a future date or a 13th month in a date field).


Some questions add dropdowns to limit options for easier completion by applicants and cleaner data for underwriters.

Results

Very few data points were being acurately tracked prior to the redesigned process, so most findings cannot be directly compared to the previous iteration. That said, completion rates stayed very high while seeing a reduction in errors upon submission.
